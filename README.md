# Online BookStore SQL Analysis

## Project Overview

Designed and analyzed a relational database for an online bookstore using PostgreSQL.

## Database Schema

Books
Customers
Orders

## Skills Used

- PostgreSQL
- Database Design
- Joins
- Aggregations
- GROUP BY
- HAVING
- ORDER BY
- Subqueries
- Business Analysis

## Key Business Questions

1. What is the total inventory available across all books?
Business Purpose:
Assess overall stock availability and support inventory planning decisions.

2.Which orders generated significant revenue?
Business Purpose:
Identify high-value transactions that contribute substantially to total sales.

3. Which book has the lowest stock level?
Business Purpose:
Identify books requiring immediate replenishment to prevent stockouts.

4. What is the total revenue generated from all orders?
Business Purpose:
Measure overall business performance and sales success.

5.Which genres generate the highest sales volume?
Business Purpose:
Understand customer preferences and optimize inventory allocation.

6.Which authors generate the highest sales volume?
Business Purpose:
Measure author popularity and inform future inventory decisions.

7.How much inventory remains after fulfilling all orders?
Business Purpose:
Support inventory management and replenishment planning.

8.How does revenue accumulate over time?
Business Purpose:
Track business growth and monitor sales trends.

## Key Insights

- Sales Performance
The bookstore generated measurable revenue through customer purchases across multiple genres.
A small group of customers contributed a disproportionately large share of total sales.
Repeat customers represent a valuable customer segment and should be targeted with retention programs.

- Product Performance
Certain books emerged as bestsellers based on order frequency.
Fiction and Fantasy titles demonstrated strong customer demand.
Premium-priced books contribute higher revenue per transaction despite lower sales volumes.

- Inventory Insights
Several books have low remaining stock levels after order fulfillment.
Inventory monitoring is critical to avoid stockouts and lost sales opportunities.
Bestseller inventory should be replenished proactively.

-Revenue Trends
Running revenue analysis demonstrates consistent sales accumulation over time.
Revenue contribution analysis highlights the most profitable genres.
Revenue concentration among a few customer segments suggests opportunities for customer diversification.

## Business Recommendations
### Inventory Management
Replenish books with critically low stock levels.
Maintain higher stock levels for bestselling titles.
### Customer Retention
Introduce loyalty programs for repeat customers.
Offer personalized recommendations to high-spending customers.
### Marketing Strategy
Focus promotions on top-performing genres.
Target high-revenue cities and regions with localized campaigns.
### Revenue Growth
Expand inventory within genres contributing the highest revenue.
Promote premium books to increase average order value.
### Data-Driven Decision Making
Continuously monitor sales trends and inventory levels using SQL dashboards and reports.
Track customer spending patterns to improve forecasting and customer segmentation.
